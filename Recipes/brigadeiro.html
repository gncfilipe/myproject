Brigadeiro

Pastel de nata is a traditional Portuguese egg custard tart pastry, known for its flaky, layered crust and creamy, slightly sweet, and spiced custard filling.

Often dusted with cinnamon and/or powdered sugar, it's a beloved treat enjoyed with coffee or tea.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of fresh puff pastry
  • 25 g of sifted wheat flour
  • 1 tablespoon sifted cornstarch
  • 175 g sugar
  • 250 ml of milk
  • 125 ml of water
  • Peel of 1 lemon
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 5 egg yolks

Preparation:

  1. Preheat the oven to 250ºC.

  2. Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface with a rolling pin until it's slightly thinner.

    Roll tightly around the longest part of the rectangle and cut into 30 equal segments.

  3. Place each one into a small mold and, with moistened fingers, stretch the dough until it comes slightly over the edge of each mold. Set aside.

  4. Place the milk, water, cinnamon stick, lemon peel, sugar, sifted flour, and cornstarch in a small saucepan.

    Heat over low heat, stirring constantly, until thickened.

  5. Add a ladleful of the cream to the beaten egg yolks, stirring constantly. Place the egg yolks in the pan and mix well.

  6. Remove the lemon peel and cinnamon stick and let cool. Pour the filling into the molds until ⅔ full and bake immediately for 12 minutes.

  7. As soon as you remove the cream from the oven, sprinkle it with a little cold water. Serve warm, sprinkled with ground cinnamon.
